Preface

This Seventh Edition illustrates the growing importance of intellectual property transactions to business and the resulting attention such transactions receive in legislative, regulatory and judicial areas. It was 13 years between the first and second editions of this text; now this edition is entirely revised to reflect the evolution in the field since the last edition was published in 2008. The new treatment of patent ownership and acquisition issues reflects the Stanford v. Roche decision and recent interest in portfolio acquisitions. The new chapter on enforcement of licensed patents reflects the continuing uncertainty as to a licensee’s standing to sue. Increased concern over infringement liability to non-practicing entities necessitated an expanded discussion of indemnity provisions. The new chapter on bankruptcy reflects the importance of intellectual property assets in a bankrupt debtor’s estate. Finally, the updated chapter on the intersection of competition law and licensing reflects the continuing evolution of that area and in particular the recent decisions relating to reverse payment settlements of Abbreviated New Drug Application (ANDA) litigation.

The computer disk provided with this Seventh Edition provides access to a set of form paragraphs that can be incorporated, with careful editing, into a license agreement being prepared by the reader. The form paragraphs are indexed to the text which provides the rationale and legal support concerning whether a particular form paragraph is appropriate to accomplish the drafter’s specific goals. The computer disk also includes the form agreements contained in the appendices.

The Seventh Edition reflects our experience in counseling clients and in teaching law school and continuing legal education courses on licensing. The changes to the forms and samples provided reflect legal developments encountered since the last edition. As with all legal documents, the form and content of license agreements reflect the legal concepts involved in transfer of rights to patents and know-how. Thus, before beginning a clause-by-clause discussion of negotiating and drafting techniques, the fundamental legal concepts are addressed in the first six chapters. While incorporating this new material into the Seventh Edition, we have attempted to preserve the concise nature of the earlier editions.
