Form G1—Confidential Disclosure Agreement for Manufacture of Products for Discloser by Disclosee

CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, dated ___________, 20__, is between ABC Company, a Maryland corporation having a principal place of business at Baltimore, MD (hereafter ABC Company), and XYZ Company, a company having a place of business at ___________ (hereafter XYZ Company).

WHEREAS ABC Company owns certain Confidential Information, as defined herein, which may relate to the design, manufacture, or use of one or more products that ABC Company desires to purchase;

WHEREAS XYZ Company wishes to sell one or more of the products to ABC Company and desires to receive from ABC Company any Confidential Information that ABC Company deems necessary to permit XYZ Company to design, manufacture, or sell the products to ABC Company; and

WHEREAS ABC Company is willing to disclose to XYZ Company such necessary Confidential Information provided XYZ Company preserves the confidential nature of the information and uses it solely for purposes of this Agreement.

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:[1]

1. “Confidential Information”[2] as used in this Agreement means all technical or business information disclosed by ABC Company to XYZ Company pursuant to this Agreement that is identified at the time of disclosure or within thirty (30) days thereafter as being confidential and proprietary to ABC Company. No information will be regarded as Confidential Information if XYZ Company can show by competent proof that such information

a. was, at the time of disclosure to XYZ Company, already known by XYZ Company, as shown by written records in the possession of XYZ Company;

b. was at the time of disclosure, or subsequently became, through no fault of XYZ Company, known to the general public through publication or otherwise; or

c. was, subsequent to disclosure to XYZ Company, lawfully and independently received by XYZ Company from a third party who had the right to disclose it without restriction.

Specific aspects or details of Confidential Information shall not be deemed to be within the public domain or in the possession of XYZ Company merely because the Confidential Information is embraced by general disclosures in the public domain or in the possession of XYZ Company. In addition, any combination of Confidential Information shall not be considered in the public domain or in the possession of XYZ Company merely because individual elements thereof are in the public domain or in the possession of XYZ Company unless the combination and its principles are in the public domain or in the possession of XYZ Company.

2. ABC Company, at its discretion, may disclose to XYZ Company any Confidential Information that ABC Company, in its reasonable judgment, believes is sufficient to enable XYZ Company to design, manufacture, or sell products that ABC Company wishes to purchase. ABC Company also may cause such disclosures to be made to XYZ Company on behalf of ABC Company by third parties who are under obligations of confidentiality to ABC Company; such disclosures from third parties shall be deemed to be disclosures by ABC Company.

3. In consideration of each and every disclosure of Confidential Information by ABC Company to XYZ Company, XYZ Company agrees

a. to treat as confidential and to preserve the confidentiality of all Confidential Information disclosed by ABC Company to XYZ Company;

b. to use any and all Confidential Information solely in connection with the design, manufacture, or sale of products to ABC Company and for no other purpose;

c. to make no disclosures of any Confidential Information to any party other than officers and employees of XYZ Company;

d. to limit access to Confidential Information to those officers and employees of XYZ Company having a reasonable need for such information and being bound by a written obligation to XYZ Company to maintain such information confidential; and

e. to maintain in confidence any information regarding the nature or scope of any transaction between ABC Company and XYZ Company, except to the extent such information must be disclosed pursuant to law, and then only after notifying ABC Company of such requirement.

Any obligation imposed by this paragraph 3 may be waived in writing by ABC Company as to particular Confidential Information and to a particular use or disclosure. Any such waiver will have a one-time effect and will not apply to any subsequent situation regardless of its similarity.

4. All Confidential Information will remain the property of ABC Company, and upon request of ABC Company, XYZ Company will promptly return to ABC Company all Confidential Information, or any part or reproduction thereof.

5. The obligations of XYZ Company and each employee and officer of XYZ Company under this Agreement will expire five (5) years from the date of this Agreement.

6. This Agreement is subject to the laws (excluding conflicts rules) of the State of Maryland.

7. The terms and provisions of this Agreement will inure to the benefit of ABC Company’s successors and assigns and will be binding on XYZ Company and XYZ Company’s successors and assigns. This paragraph notwithstanding, XYZ Company may not disclose any Confidential Information to any successor or assign absent prior written consent of ABC Company.

8. XYZ Company understands and agrees that no right or license under any patent, patent application, or know-how is granted to XYZ Company or any other person by this Agreement or by any disclosure of any Confidential Information.[3]

9. All notices pursuant to this Agreement will be effective five (5) days after mailing, postage paid first class, to the address first written above, or on receipt at the address first written above if delivered by hand or by facsimile.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement on the date first written above.

Form G2—Confidential Evaluation Agreement with Option Terms

INITIAL CONFIDENTIAL EVALUATION AGREEMENT[4]

This is an Agreement by and between ABC Engineering Company, a Michigan corporation, located at ___________ (hereafter “ABC”), and XYZ Corporation, a ___________ corporation, having a principal place of business at ___________ (hereafter “XYZ”).

WHEREAS, ABC has developed and owns proprietary technical and commercial information concerning the design, manufacture, operation, and commercial exploitation of a ___________ (“System”), the System comprising a ___________ unit (“Primary Unit”) and circuits connecting the Primary Unit to the space being treated (“Secondary Unit”). The proprietary information includes inventions, patent applications, trade secrets, know-how, and working prototypes and components thereof, all of which ABC considers to be confidential and proprietary;

WHEREAS, XYZ is considering a license from ABC under proprietary information related to manufacture and commercial exploitation of the Primary Unit and components thereof (“Technology”), and, as a part of that consideration, XYZ desires the opportunity to evaluate the Technology and the System on a confidential basis;

WHEREAS, ABC is willing to disclose the Technology to XYZ on a nonexclusive basis solely for an evaluation under terms and conditions that will permit XYZ initially to obtain an overview of the Technology sufficient for a decision on whether to seek a negotiated option for a license and a subsequent detailed review of the Technology while preserving to ABC the confidential, proprietary nature of the Technology; and

WHEREAS, while ABC may provide others with a similar initial disclosure under similar terms and conditions, ABC is willing to negotiate for and to grant an exclusive option for an exclusive license to manufacture and sell the Primary Unit.

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

1. This Agreement shall be effective on the date of signature of the last party to sign (“Effective Date”).

2. The Technology pertaining to the Primary Unit is regarded as confidential by ABC and is to be proffered to XYZ as such. All Technology disclosed by ABC will be received and held in confidence by XYZ, and XYZ will not disclose the Technology to anyone and will not use the Technology for any purpose whatsoever other than its evaluation pursuant to this Agreement.

3. XYZ will designate in writing not more than three evaluators who may be employees of or consultants contractually retained by XYZ and who will be reasonably acceptable to ABC. Each evaluator will sign a copy of the acknowledgment in Attachment A hereto which will accompany the written designation by XYZ. ABC will make an initial disclosure of the Technology to the evaluators for the sole purpose of permitting them to reach an initial opinion of the commercial worth of the Technology and to make a recommendation to XYZ.

4. The initial disclosure of the Technology will be made at ABC’s facility during not more than a three-day period to be mutually agreed upon by the parties. ABC will disclose to the evaluators the details of the Primary Unit including design and manufacturing information, manufacturing and operation cost estimations, and other information necessary to permit them to make an initial recommendation to XYZ concerning the Technology. The content and scope of the initial disclosure will be consistent with that outlined in Attachment B hereto. XYZ recognizes that significant aspects of the Technology are likely to be disclosed orally and therefore agrees that all or any part of the initial disclosure may be videotaped by ABC. Neither the videotape nor any other documents disclosed during the initial disclosure may be removed from the ABC facility.

5. Although this Agreement does not contemplate disclosure for evaluation of proprietary information regarding the Secondary Unit, the parties recognize that some such information may be disclosed inadvertently during performance under this Agreement. Accordingly, to the extent such information is disclosed and concurrently or later identified as such in accordance with the terms of this Agreement, the information will be subject to this Agreement as if a part of the Technology.

6. After the initial disclosure, the evaluators may disclose to XYZ their opinion and recommendation, but may not disclose details of the Technology.

7. XYZ and the evaluators will use reasonable commercial efforts, including efforts fully commensurate with those employed by XYZ for the protection of its own trade secrets, to protect ABC’s Technology disclosed to XYZ pursuant to this Agreement. XYZ and the evaluators will be separately and collectively responsible for maintaining the Technology in confidence.

8. ABC agrees that the confidentiality and use provisions of this Agreement will not apply to any Technology

a. that appears in issued patents or printed publications in integrated form or that otherwise is or becomes generally known in the trade other than through the fault of XYZ;

b. that XYZ can show by written records was in XYZ’s possession prior to its disclosure by ABC; or

c. that XYZ can show by written records came into its possession, without covenants of secrecy, from another party who is under no confidentiality obligation to ABC.

Specific aspects or details of the Technology will not be deemed to be published, generally known in the trade, or otherwise within the public domain, or to be in the possession of XYZ, merely because the aspects of the Technology are embraced by general disclosures in the public domain or in XYZ’s possession. In addition, any combination of aspects of the Technology will not be considered in the public domain or in the possession of XYZ merely because individual elements thereof are in the public domain or in XYZ’s possession unless the combination and its principles are in the public domain or in XYZ’s possession. The burden of proving these exceptions to the confidentiality and use provisions of this Agreement resides with XYZ.

9. In consideration for the opportunity of an initial confidential evaluation pursuant to the terms of this Agreement, XYZ will pay to ABC on execution of this Agreement a fee of five thousand dollars ($5,000). The fee shall be nonrefundable but may be credited against the option fee contemplated by paragraph 12(f) of this Agreement.

10. If, after consideration of the initial disclosure and recommendation of the evaluators, XYZ decides it is not interested in further evaluation or a license, this Agreement will terminate except that the provisions of paragraphs 2, 5, 7, and 8 hereof will continue in full force and effect.

11. If XYZ is sufficiently interested in the Technology and the System to justify further evaluation, XYZ will provide written notice thereof to ABC within fifteen (15) business days after the end of the disclosure period agreed upon pursuant to paragraph 4 hereof. ABC, on receipt of such notice, will negotiate in good faith with XYZ for a license agreement and an option therefor.

12. The option subject to negotiation pursuant to paragraph 11 hereof will include at least the following provisions:

a. Within thirty (30) days after execution of the option, ABC will furnish to XYZ the proprietary documentation and tangible items (including prototypes) necessary to permit XYZ to conduct a thorough evaluation of the design, manufacture, and operation of the System and of XYZ’s interest in the license under the Technology. ABC will provide to XYZ, in duplicate, a complete inventory of all documentation and tangible items furnished (“Inventory”). XYZ, by written notation and dated signature on the Inventory, will acknowledge receipt of all items received and note those items not received, if any. If XYZ determines to continue its evaluation away from the ABC facility, XYZ will bear the cost and responsibility for shipment. No shipment of any material from the ABC facility may be made until ABC receives from XYZ a signed copy of the Inventory.

b. Prototypes included within the Technology may be the property of the U.S. Government pursuant to Government funding agreements. Accordingly, during the evaluation, XYZ will not subject any prototype or component thereof to destructive testing.

c. Each document, item, videotape, program, or other material provided by ABC to XYZ, if proprietary to ABC, will contain the following legend on each page thereof:

CONFIDENTIAL DATA. PROPRIETARY TO ABC ENGINEERING COMPANY. FURNISHED FOR EVALUATION PURPOSES ONLY. NOT TO BE COPIED OR DISCLOSED OR USED FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN EVALUATION.

d. XYZ will restrict disclosure of the Technology to the minimum number of officers, employees, and outside consultants of XYZ required to enable XYZ to further appraise the value of the Technology and the System. Each such officer, employee, and outside consultant, before the disclosure takes place, will sign an acknowledgment in the form set forth in Attachment A.

e. Confidentiality and nonuse provisions as set forth in paragraphs 2, 7, and 8 of this Agreement will be included in the option agreement.

f. XYZ will pay to ABC an option fee of $100,000, half payable on execution of the option agreement and half payable on signature by XYZ of the Inventory. The option fee will be nonrefundable, but XYZ may credit the option fee against the license fee required by the license agreement.

g. XYZ will have ninety (90) days after the date of signature on the Inventory in which to evaluate the Technology and the System. Exercise of the option for a license will be effective on receipt by ABC of the negotiated license executed by XYZ.

h. If XYZ fails to exercise the option pursuant to its terms, XYZ, within ten (10) days after expiration of the option, will

i. return all documents and tangible items it has received from ABC under the option agreement;

ii. assume the liability for any damage to tangible items returned;

iii. forward to ABC all copies, summaries, records, descriptions, modifications, drawings, photographs, videotapes, computer-based data, and adaptations of the documents and tangible items received from ABC and all other documents or items that contain any information derived from the documents and tangible items received from ABC;

iv. irretrievably erase all computer memories containing any information received from ABC or derived from information received from ABC; and

v. not manufacture or sell any system comparable to the System for five (5) years after the effective date of the option agreement.

13. The license agreement will be consistent with the terms and conditions set forth in Attachment C hereto.

Form G3—Confidential Disclosure Agreement (One Way Out)[5]

CONFIDENTIAL DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, effective on the date of final execution by all parties (“Effective Date”), is by and between ABC Inc., a Texas corporation whose principal address is ______________________________ (“ABC”), and ___________, a ___________ corporation having offices at _______________________ (“Confidant”) (each “Party” and both “Parties”).

RECITAL

ABC owns certain Confidential Information, as defined herein, which Confidant desires to receive solely for the Purpose as defined herein. ABC is willing to disclose to Confidant such Confidential Information for the Purpose, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

AGREEMENT

In consideration of the foregoing and of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Definitions. As used in this agreement:

(a) “Purpose” means Confidant’s evaluation and analysis of possible business relationships between the Parties relating to development and exploitation of _____________________________.

(b) “Confidential Information” means information, including without limitation, technology, designs, processes, machines, manufactures, compositions of matter, know-how, computer programs, product designs, and market and business plans, that is disclosed pursuant to this agreement by ABC to Confidant. No information will be regarded as Confidential Information which Confidant can show by competent proof:

(i) is at the time of disclosure, or thereafter becomes, a part of the public domain without breach of this Agreement by Confidant;

(ii) is lawfully in the possession of Confidant prior to disclosure by ABC as shown by written records; or

(iii) is lawfully disclosed to Confidant by a third party which did not acquire the same under an obligation of confidentiality to ABC.

2. Disclosure of Confidential Information. ABC may disclose to Confidant such Confidential Information as ABC, in its reasonable judgment, believes necessary to enable Confidant to accomplish the Purpose.[6]

3. Protection of Confidential Information. In consideration of each and every disclosure of Confidential Information by ABC to Confidant, Confidant will:

(a) treat as confidential and preserve the confidentiality of all Confidential Information disclosed to it;

(b) make no use of any Confidential Information disclosed to it except in connection with the Purpose without the prior written consent of ABC; and

(c) make no disclosures of any Confidential Information to any person not an officer or employee of Confidant without the prior written consent of ABC.

4. Employees. Confidant will limit disclosure of Confidential Information to officers and employees who need to know to accomplish the Purpose and will advise each such officer and employee who receives any Confidential Information of the confidential nature of such information.

5. Compelled Disclosure. If disclosure of Confidential Information by Confidant is required by order of a competent court or administrative agency, Confidant may comply with that order as long as it gives ABC prior written notice sufficiently in advance to permit ABC to seek an appropriate protective order and cooperates with ABC (at ABC’s expense) in seeking that order.

6. Return of Confidential Information. Upon request from ABC, Confidant must promptly return to ABC all Confidential Information in Confidant’s possession and any other documents, databases, or tangible items constituting an embodiment of or containing information derived from Confidential Information.

7. Period of Protection. The obligations of Confidant and each officer and employee of Confidant under this agreement regarding Confidential Information disclosed to Confidant or any of its officers or employees will expire thirty-six (36) months after the date of expiration or termination of this Agreement.

8. Term and Termination. The term of this Agreement will be from the Effective Date to the first anniversary thereof and on such date will automatically renew for subsequent one-year terms unless a Party, by written notice to the other Party not less than 30 days prior to such renewal, elects not to renew. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time on written notice to the other Party. If not renewed, this Agreement will expire on the anniversary date. If terminated, the date of termination will be five business days after the date of the notice of termination.

9. Survival. The obligations of confidentiality and non-use as set forth in this Agreement will survive for the period of protection provided in paragraph 7.

10. No Obligation, No License. Nothing in this Agreement obligates ABC to disclose anything to Confidant. No right or license under any patent, patent application, or know-how is granted to Confidant or any other person by this Agreement or by any disclosure of Confidential Information hereunder, except for the right of Confidant to use solely for the Purpose any Confidential Information disclosed to it.

11. Choice of Law. This Agreement is acknowledged to have been made in and shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, U.S.A.

12. Amendment. No amendment to this Agreement will be valid unless made in writing and signed by an officer of each Party.

13. Assignment. Confidant may not assign this Agreement or the rights and obligations herein without the prior written approval of ABC. ABC may assign this Agreement or the rights provided hereunder in conjunction with assignment of the Confidential Information.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties hereto have caused this Agreement to be executed by their duly authorized representatives effective as of the day and year first above written.

Form G4—Confidential Disclosure Agreement (One Way In)[7]

CONFIDENTIAL DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT, effective on the date of final execution by all parties (“Effective Date”), is by and between ABC, Inc., a Texas corporation whose principal address is _______________________________ (“ABC”) , and ____________, a ____________ corporation having offices at ____________ (“Discloser”) (each “Party” and both “Parties”).

RECITAL

Discloser owns certain Confidential Information, as defined herein, which ABC desires to receive solely for the Purpose as defined herein. Discloser is willing to disclose to ABC such Confidential Information for the Purpose, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

AGREEMENT

In consideration of the foregoing and of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Definitions. As used in this agreement:

(a) “Purpose” means ABC’s evaluation and analysis of possible business relationships between the Parties relating to development and exploitation of ____________________________________.

(b) “Confidential Information” means information, including without limitation, technology, designs, processes, machines, manufactures, compositions of matter, know-how, computer programs, product designs, and market and business plans, that is disclosed pursuant to this agreement by Discloser to ABC in tangible form, marked confidential, or, if disclosed verbally or visually, identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and reduced to a tangible form marked confidential and transmitted to ABC within thirty (30) days after the original disclosure, and that relates to the Purpose.[8] No information shall be regarded as Confidential Information which ABC can show by competent proof:

(i) is at the time of disclosure, or thereafter becomes, a part of the public domain without breach of this Agreement by ABC;

(ii) is lawfully in the possession of ABC prior to disclosure by Discloser as shown by written records;

(iii) is lawfully disclosed to ABC by a third party which did not acquire the same under an obligation of confidentiality to Discloser; or

(iv) is independently developed by ABC without use of Discloser’s Confidential Information.[9]

(c) “Related Company” means any company that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party, where “control” and cognates thereof mean direct or indirect ownership of more than 50% of equity in a company or possession by contract or otherwise of the power to appoint a majority of a company’s board of directors or to control the operations of the company.

2. Protection of Confidential Information. In consideration of each and every disclosure of Confidential Information by Discloser to ABC, ABC agrees to:

(a) treat as confidential and preserve the confidentiality of all Confidential Information disclosed to it;

(b) make no use of any Confidential Information disclosed to it except in connection with the Purpose without the prior written consent of Discloser; and

(c) make no disclosures of any Confidential Information to any person not an officer or employee of ABC without the prior written consent of the Discloser, provided, however, that ABC may disclose Confidential Information to its consultants or Related Companies that agree to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.[10]

3. Employees. ABC shall advise each of its officers, employees, consultants, and Related Companies who receive Confidential Information under this Agreement of the confidential nature of such information.

4. Compelled Disclosure. If disclosure of Confidential Information by ABC is required by order of a competent court or administrative agency, ABC may comply with that order as long as it gives Discloser prior written notice sufficiently in advance to permit Discloser to seek an appropriate protective order and cooperates with Discloser (at Discloser’s expense) in seeking that order.

5. Return of Confidential Information. Upon request from Discloser, ABC must promptly return to Discloser or certify in writing that it has destroyed all Confidential Information in ABC’s possession; provided, however, that one copy of such Confidential Information may be retained by ABC to preserve a record of the same.[11]

6. Period of Protection. The obligations of ABC and each officer, employee, consultant, and Related Company of ABC under this Agreement regarding Confidential Information disclosed to ABC or any of its officers, employees, consultants, and Related Companies during a Term hereof will expire twenty-four (24) months[12] after the date of expiration or termination of the applicable Term of this Agreement.

7. Term and Termination. This Agreement will be in effect from the Effective Date to the first anniversary thereof (a “Term”) and on such date will automatically expire. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time on written notice to the other Party. If the Agreement is terminated, the date of termination will be five business days after the date of the notice of termination.

8. Renewal. The Parties may agree in writing to renew this Agreement for an additional one-year Term. Absent a Party’s desire to modify a provision, renewal may be effected by written notice from one Party to the other. Absent objection by the other Party, a new agreement having the same terms as this Agreement will be effective on the date of such notice. Confidential Information disclosed during a Term of this Agreement will be subject to the obligations of paragraphs 2, 3, 4, and 5 of this Agreement until expiration of the period set forth in paragraph 6 hereof.

9. No Obligation, No License. No right or license under any patent, patent application, or know-how is granted to ABC or any other person by this Agreement or by any disclosure of Confidential Information hereunder, except for the right of ABC to use solely for the Purpose any Confidential Information disclosed to it.

10. Choice of Law. This Agreement is acknowledged to have been made in and shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, U.S.A.

11. Amendment. No amendment to this Agreement will be valid unless made in writing and signed by an officer of each Party.

12. Assignment. Neither this Agreement nor the rights and obligations herein may be assigned by a Party to anyone without the written permission of the other Party, provided, however, that either Party, without the other Party’s permission, may assign this Agreement to a third party in conjunction with sale to the third party of substantially all of the assets of the assigning Party associated with the Purpose. The assignment will be effective upon receipt by the non-assigning Party of written agreement by the third party to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

Form G5—Confidential Disclosure Agreement (Mutual)[13]

Mutual Confidential Disclosure Agreement

THIS AGREEMENT, effective on the date of final execution by all parties (“Effective Date”), is by and between ABC, Inc., a Texas corporation whose principal address is ________________________________, and XYZ Corporation, an Illinois corporation having offices at ________________________ (each “Party” and both “Parties”).

RECITAL

Each Party owns certain Confidential Information, as defined herein, which the other Party desires to receive solely for the Purpose as defined herein. Each Party is willing to disclose to the other Party such Confidential Information for the Purpose, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

AGREEMENT

In consideration of the foregoing and of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Definitions. As used in this agreement:

(a) “Purpose” means each Party’s evaluation and analysis of possible business relationships between the Parties relating to development and exploitation of ______________________________.

(b) “Confidential Information” means information, including without limitation, technology, designs, processes, machines, manufactures, compositions of matter, know-how, computer programs, product designs, and market and business plans, that is disclosed pursuant to this agreement by a Party (“Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (“Receiving Party”) in tangible form, marked confidential, or, if disclosed verbally or visually, identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and reduced to a tangible form marked confidential and transmitted to the Receiving Party within thirty (30) days after the original disclosure, and that relates to the Purpose. No information shall be regarded as Confidential Information which the Receiving Party can show by competent proof:

(i) is at the time of disclosure, or thereafter becomes, a part of the public domain without breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(ii) is lawfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party as shown by written records;

(iii) is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party which did not acquire the same under an obligation of confidentiality to the Disclosing Party; or

(iv) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information.

(c) “Related Company” means any company that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party, where “control” and cognates thereof mean direct or indirect ownership of more than 50% of equity in a company or possession by contract or otherwise of the power to appoint a majority of a company’s board of directors or to control the operations of the company.

2. Protection of Confidential Information. In consideration of each and every disclosure of Confidential Information by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, the Receiving Party shall:

(a) treat as confidential and preserve the confidentiality of all Confidential Information disclosed to it;

(b) make no use of any Confidential Information disclosed to it except in connection with the Purpose without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party; and

(c) make no disclosure of any Confidential Information to any person not an officer or employee of the Receiving Party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, provided, however, that the Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its consultants or Related Companies that agree to be bound by similar obligations of confidentiality. The Receiving Party will use the same degree of care in treating as confidential and preserving the confidentiality of all Confidential Information disclosed to it as it employs with respect to its own proprietary information of like importance.

3. Compelled Disclosure. If disclosure by a Receiving Party is required by order of a competent court or administrative agency, that Receiving Party may comply with that order as long as it gives the Disclosing Party prior written notice sufficiently in advance to permit the Disclosing Party to seek an appropriate protective order and cooperates with the Disclosing Party (at the Disclosing Party’s expense) in seeking that order.

4. Return of Confidential Information. Upon request from the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party must promptly return to the Disclosing Party or certify in writing that it has destroyed all Confidential Information, or any item thereof if such is requested, having come into Receiving Party’s possession; provided, however, that one copy of such Confidential Information may be retained by the Receiving Party to preserve a record of the same.

5. Period of Protection. The obligations of the Receiving Party and each officer, employee, consultant, and Related Company of the Receiving Party under this agreement regarding Confidential Information disclosed to the Receiving Party or any of its officers, employees, consultants, or Related Companies during a Term hereof will expire twenty-four (24) months after the date of expiration or termination of the applicable Term of this Agreement.

6. Term and Termination. This Agreement will be effective from the Effective Date to the first anniversary thereof (a “Term”) and on such date will automatically renew for a subsequent one-year Term unless a Party, by written notice to the other Party not less than 30 days prior to such renewal, elects not to renew. Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time on written notice to the other Party. If not renewed, this Agreement will expire on expiration of the then current Term. If the Agreement is terminated, the date of termination will be five business days after the date of the notice of termination. Confidential Information disclosed during a Term of this Agreement will be subject to the obligations of paragraphs 2, 3, and 4 of this Agreement until expiration of the period set forth in paragraph 5 hereof.

7. No Obligation, No License. Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to disclose anything to the other Party. No right or license under any patent, patent application, or know-how is granted to either Party or any other person by this Agreement or by any disclosure of Confidential Information hereunder, except for the right of the Receiving Party to use solely for the Purpose any Confidential Information disclosed to it.

8. Choice of Law. This Agreement is acknowledged to have been made in and shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, U.S.A.

9. Amendment. No amendment to this Agreement will be valid unless made in writing and signed by a duly authorized representative of each Party.

10. Assignment. Neither this Agreement nor the rights and obligations herein may be assigned by a Party to anyone without the written permission of the other Party, provided, however, that either Party, without the other Party’s permission, may assign this Agreement to a third party in conjunction with sale to the third party of substantially all of the assets of the assigning Party associated with the Purpose.
