Chapter 30: Export Laws and Regulations of the United States

Form 30.00A—Written Assurance of Foreign Licensee That It Will Not Reexport Technology and Products of the Technology If Subject to U.S. Security Regulations

[Receiving party] hereby assures [exporting party] that it will not (1) reexport or release [Insert a clear and accurate description of the technology, software or source code being exported under License Exception TSR] received from [exporting party] or (2) export the direct products of such [technology, software, or source code], to any country in Country Group E:1 or in Country Group D:1, as specified in Supp. No. 1 to §738 of the EAR and as modified from time to time, or to any national or resident of any such country, unless prior written authorization is obtained from the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), U.S. Department of Commerce, or the reexport or export is otherwise encompassed by a License Exception set forth in §740 of the EAR.

[Receiving party] further assures [exporting party] that if the direct product of any such technology received from [exporting party] is a complete plant or any major component of a plant, [receiving party] will not export the direct product of the plant or major component thereof, if such foreign-produced direct product is subject to national security controls as identified on the Commerce Control List set forth in Supp. No. 1 to §774 of the Export Administration Regulations (15 C.F.R. §730 et seq.) or subject to State Department Controls under the U.S. Munitions List (22 C.F.R. Part 121), to any country in Country Group E:2 or Country Group D:1, as specified in Supp. No. 1 to §738 of the EAR and as modified from time to time, or to any national or resident of any such country, unless prior written authorization is obtained from the BIS or the reexport or export is otherwise encompassed by a License Exception set forth in §740 of the EAR.

[Receiving party] understands that its obligations under this provision and under U.S. export control laws and regulations governing access to and use of goods, technology, source code, and/or software received at any time under this Agreement survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

Form 30.00B—Generic Written Assurance of Foreign Licensee That It Will Not Reexport Technology and Products of the Technology If Subject to U.S. Security Regulations

[Receiving party] hereby assures [exporting party] that it will not (1) reexport or release any technology, software, or source code received from [exporting party] that is subject to national security controls and identified on the Commerce Control List (CCL) set forth in Supp. No. 1 to §774 of the Export Administration Regulations (15 C.F.R. §730 et seq.) (EAR); or (2) export the direct products of such technology, software, or source code, if such foreign-produced direct product is subject to national security controls as identified on the CCL, to any country in Country Group E:1 or in Country Group D:1, as specified in Supp. No. 1 to §738 of the EAR and as modified from time to time, or to any national or resident of any such country, unless prior written authorization is obtained from the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS), U.S. Department of Commerce, or the reexport or export is otherwise encompassed by a License Exception set forth in §740 of the EAR.

[Receiving party] further assures [exporting party] that if the direct product of any technology received from [exporting party] is a complete plant or any major component of a plant, [receiving party] will not export the direct product of the plant or major component thereof, if such foreign-produced direct product is subject to national security controls as identified on the CCL or subject to State Department Controls under the U.S. Munitions List (22 C.F.R. Part 121), to any country in Country Group E:1 or Country Group D:1, as specified in Supp. No. 1 to §738 of the EAR and as modified from time to time, or to any national or resident of any such country, unless prior written authorization is obtained from the BIS or the reexport or export is otherwise encompassed by a License Exception set forth in §740 of the EAR.

[Receiving party] understands that its obligations under this provision and under U.S. export control laws and regulations governing access to and use of goods, technology, source code, and/or software received at any time under this Agreement survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

Form 30.00C—Export Control Notice

All obligations to furnish goods, technology, or software source code under this Agreement are subject to U.S. Export Control Laws and Regulations. [Exporting party] agrees to comply fully with all applicable laws and regulations before exporting any goods, technology, or software to [receiving party]. [Receiving party] recognizes that any reexport of such goods, technology, or software source code and/or the export of the direct product of such items may be subject to U.S. export control laws and regulations and agrees to comply with such laws and regulations prior to undertaking any such reexports or exports, to the extent, of course, such actions are expressly authorized under the terms of this Agreement. [Receiving party] recognizes that its obligations to comply with U.S. export control laws and regulations survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.
