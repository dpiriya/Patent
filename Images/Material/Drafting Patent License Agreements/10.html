Chapter 10: The Granting Clause; Elements of the Grant; Definition of Licensed Subject Matter

Form 10.01A—Definitions of “Subsidiary” (Alternate), “Controlled Company,” and “Affiliate” (Alternate)

1. Subsidiary of a party means any corporation over fifty percent (50%) of the voting stock of which is directly or indirectly owned or controlled by such party.

2. Subsidiary of a party means a corporation, company, or other entity more than fifty percent (50%) of whose outstanding securities representing the right, other than as affected by events of default, to vote for the election of directors or other governing authorities is now or hereafter owned or controlled, directly or indirectly, by such party, but such corporation, company, or other entity shall be deemed to be a Subsidiary only so long as such ownership or control exists.

3. Controlled Company of a party means

(a) any Subsidiary, and

(b) any corporation, company, or other entity, not a Subsidiary, at least ___% (e.g., 50% or less) of the voting stock of which is directly or indirectly owned or controlled by such party, provided such party also has either (i) the irrevocable right to name a majority of the members of the governing board of such corporation, or (ii) effective managerial control by virtue of a management agreement entered into with such corporation.

4. An Affiliate of a party means any person or legal entity that is a general licensee of such party in the field of this agreement and that has a contract with such party entitling it to receive continuing technical services from the party, but any such person shall be deemed to be an Affiliate only so long as it has such a contract and continues to be such a licensee.

5. “Affiliate” means a person or entity that directly or indirectly through one or more intermediates, controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with the person or entity specified. For the purpose of this definition only, “control” and cognates thereof mean with respect to an entity, the direct or indirect ownership of (a) at least fifty percent (50%) or more of the capital stock or share capital entitled to vote for the election of directors of the entity (or such lesser percentage which is the maximum allowed to be owned by a foreign corporation in a particular jurisdiction) or (b) at least fifty percent (50%) of equity or voting interest of the entity (or such lesser percentage which is the maximum allowed to be owned by a foreign corporation in a particular jurisdiction) or (c) the ability to otherwise direct the management and operations of the entity. An entity will be an Affiliate for purposes of this Agreement only so long as it satisfies the definition set forth herein.

Form 10.01B—Subsidiaries Receive License Only Upon Notification to Licensor

This license shall extend to subsidiaries present and future, of licensee who notify licensor in writing that they accept the terms, including the obligations, of this agreement respecting such license.

Form 10.01C—Definition of Licensed Patents (to Include Inventions Made Within Five Years of Execution)

1. Five-Year Period means the period beginning with the date of execution of this Agreement and ending five years after such date.

2. Licensed Patents means all issued U.S. patents claiming (A) inventions first conceived and first actually or constructively reduced to practice at any time during the Five-Year Period in the course of employment by employees of Licensor who are employed to do research, development, or other inventive work; (B) inventions reduced to practice either actually or constructively prior to the termination of the Five-Year Period and owned or controlled by Licensor at any time during the Five-Year Period; and (C) inventions reduced to practice either actually or constructively prior to the termination of the Five-Year Period, with respect to which and to the extent to which Licensor at any time during the Five-Year Period may have the right to grant the licenses and rights agreed to be granted by Licensor in this Agreement.

Form 10.01D—Definition of Licensed Patents (including all international patents)

Licensed Patents means (a) the patents and patent applications owned or controlled by Licensor as listed in Exhibit A attached hereto, (b) all divisional, continuation, and continuation-in-part applications of any such patent applications, (c) all patents issuing from any of the foregoing applications, (d) all reissues, reexaminations, and extensions of any of the foregoing patents, and (e) all patents and patent applications anywhere in the world that, at any time, claimed priority from any of the foregoing patent applications.

Form 10.01E—Licensed Patents (in terms of products claimed)

Licensed Patents means Patent Rights owned or Controlled by Licensor at any time during the term of this Agreement that claim the manufacture, use, sale, offer for sale, or import of a Licensed Product.

Patent Rights means (a) patent applications filed in any country; (b) all patents, including supplemental protection certificates, that have issued or in the future issue from any of the foregoing, including, without limitation, utility models, design patents and certificates of invention; and (c) all divisionals, continuations, continuations-in-part, reissues, re-examination certificates, renewals, extensions, or additions to any such patents and patent applications.

Controlled and cognates thereof mean, solely with respect to Patent Rights, the ability to grant to Licensee rights of the scope granted in this Agreement without having to obtain permission of or to pay additional consideration to a third party.

Form 10.01F—Patent Expiration Provision

A patent shall be understood to expire at midnight of the date of its expiration.

Form 10.01G—Limitation of the Have-Made Grant

The license “to have made” granted in Section ____ means only that the Licensee may contract with a third party or parties to manufacture Licensed Products solely for use or sale by Licensee pursuant to the terms of this Agreement.

Form 10.01H—Definition of “Licensed Apparatus Otherwise Disposed Of”

The expression “Licensed Apparatus otherwise disposed of” as used in this Agreement means (1) Licensed Apparatus not sold but delivered by Licensee to others (including deliveries for export), regardless of the basis for compensation, if any, and (2) Licensed Apparatus put into use by Licensee for any purpose other than routine testing of such Apparatus.

Form 10.01I—Disclaimer of Contractual Restraint on Export

Nothing in this Agreement is intended to preclude the export or the sale for export of products licensed in the Agreement to be made, used, offered for sale, and sold (or “made, used, offered for sale, and sold or otherwise disposed of,” if that phraseology is employed in the basic grant), and on which royalties have been paid as provided in Article ____ of this Agreement.

Form 10.01J—Disclaimer of Contractual Restraint on Export, Reservation of Foreign Patent Rights

Nothing in this Agreement is intended to preclude the export or sale for export of products licensed in the Agreement to be sold or otherwise disposed of and on which royalties have been paid as provided in Article ____ of this Agreement, but no rights are granted or to be implied in respect to such exported products under any patent owned by Licensor or any other person in any country outside the Licensed Territory.

Form 10.01K—Required Notice on Resale Under Foreign Patents

Nothing in this Agreement is intended to preclude the export or sale for export of products licensed in the Agreement to be sold or otherwise disposed of and on which royalties have been paid as provided in Article ____ of the Agreement, but no rights are granted or to be implied in respect to such exported products under any patent of Licensor or any other person in any country outside the Licensed Territory. Licensee must notify all purchasers from it of products licensed hereunder that the products are not licensed for use or resale in any country outside the Licensed Territory in which Licensor has patent rights covering such products.

Form 10.02A—Definition of Licensed Products (Broad)

ARTICLE I

1.00 Licensed Patents means . . . .[1]

1.01 Licensed Products means any and all products that employ or are produced by the practice of inventions claimed in the Licensed Patents.

Form 10.02B—Detailed Definition of Licensed Products

Licensed Products means any and all products the manufacture, use, sale, offer for sale, or import of which, but for the license granted in this Agreement, would infringe or contribute to the infringement of a claim of a Licensed Patent.

Form 10.02C—Definition of Licensed Products (Narrowed)

1.02 Licensed Products means—

(a) Wireless communication systems and apparatus for transmitting, receiving, repeating, modifying, responding to, or utilizing information conveyed by energy propagated through space, including (without limiting the generality of the foregoing) radio broadcast, television broadcast, radio telephone, radio telegraph, and radio facsimile systems and apparatus; and

(b) Parts, components, subassemblies, circuit elements, or materials primarily designed for use in any of the systems and apparatus of (a), above, but excluding parts, components, subassemblies, circuit elements, and materials that are developed or designed primarily either (i) for general use or (ii) for specific use other than for use in such systems and apparatus.


[1]See §10.01.C, infra.
