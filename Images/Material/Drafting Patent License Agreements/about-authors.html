About the Authors

Brian G. Brunsvold, a senior counsel with Finnegan, Henderson, Farabow, Garrett & Dunner, LLP, in Washington, D.C., has had extensive hands-on licensing experience for more than 30 years. He has litigated as lead counsel in patent infringement cases before federal district courts and the United States International Trade Commission (ITC). Mr. Brunsvold has lectured on licensing issues for the ABA, ALI-ABA, Patent Resources Group, and the World Trade Center. Mr. Brunsvold has also served as an expert witness and expert consultant on licensing issues.

D. Patrick O'Reilley, also a senior counsel with Finnegan, Henderson, Farabow, Garrett & Dunner, LLP, in Washington, D.C., has extensive experience in assisting clients with transactions involving transfer of intellectual property rights. He represents clients as licensors and licensees, and negotiates and drafts agreements ranging from simple licenses to complex collaboration agreements. He has written many articles on licensing and related issues; taught licensing law at George Mason University Law School; is a regular instructor on licensing for the Patent Resources Group and for the World Intellectual Property Organization, and is active in education and leadership positions in the Licensing Executives Society.

D. Brian Kacedon, a partner with Finnegan, Henderson, Farabow, Garrett & Dunner, LLP, in Washington, D.C., has extensive experience in patent and technology licensing as well as patent litigation. He assists clients in all aspects of licensing, including establishing licensing programs, developing licensing strategies, and negotiating and drafting license agreements. He has considerable experience in developing and implementing programs for licensing pooled patents directed to standards. He frequently lectures at legal and industry workshops and conferences on licensing issues, is an Adjunct Professor at George Washington University Law School, is a regular instructor for the Patent Resources Group, and is active in leadership positions in the Licensing Executives Society.
