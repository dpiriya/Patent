Chapter 7: Issues in License Agreement Negotiation

Form 7.03—Negotiation Agreement

1. Acme and Zulu wish to discuss their respective interests in a potential agreement involving at least U.S. Patent No. ________ (“Potential Agreement”).

2. Acme and Zulu wish to ensure a free and frank discussion regarding the Potential Agreement (“Negotiations”) and therefore have agreed that their discussions, disclosures, and correspondence concerning the Potential Agreement will be subject to the following terms and conditions.

3. All information disclosed by one party to the other during or in connection with the Negotiations, whether orally or in tangible form (“Disclosed Information”), will be received by the other party in confidence, and the other party hereby agrees not to use any such Disclosed Information for any reason other than the Potential Agreement and not to disclose any such Disclosed Information to any third party. The obligations of this paragraph 3 will not apply to Disclosed Information which, through no fault of the receiving party, becomes part of the public domain or which the receiving party can show was already in its possession.

4. Neither party will use any Disclosed Information or the existence of Negotiations or this Negotiation Agreement as the basis for or evidence of:

a. in personam jurisdiction of a party or any of its related companies in any court;

b. a declaratory judgment action against a party or any of its related companies; or

c. waiver of any attorney-client privilege or attorney work product exemption.

5. Each party agrees to consider all Disclosed Information as if it were subject to Rule 408 of the Federal Rules of Evidence.

6. Each party agrees to cause its counsel and other representatives to comply with the terms of this Negotiation Agreement.
