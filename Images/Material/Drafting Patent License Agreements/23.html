Chapter 23: Bankruptcy Law Considerations

Form 23.03—Intellectual Property Agreement for Bankruptcy

The Parties agree that the Licensed Intellectual Property is “intellectual property” as defined in 11 U.S.C. §101(56) and that this Agreement is governed by 11 U.S.C. §365(n) or a comparable bankruptcy law of any other jurisdiction. The Parties further agree that, in the event of rejection of this Agreement by Licensor, its successor or trustee under 11 U.S.C. §365(a) or a comparable bankruptcy law of any other jurisdiction, Licensee may terminate this Agreement for cause under 11 U.S.C. §365(n)(1)(A) or retain its rights under 11 U.S.C. §365(n)(1)(B).

Form 23.04A—Intellectual Property Agreement for Bankruptcy

The Parties agree for purposes of Section 365(n) of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code and any similar Laws in any other country, that this Agreement grants licenses or rights to “intellectual property” as defined under Section 101(52) of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code.

Form 23.04B—Assignment Restrictions

The rights and licenses granted by Licensor in this Agreement are personal to Licensee and may not be assigned or otherwise transferred without the written consent of Licensor, which will not be unreasonably withheld, delayed, or conditioned. Licensor will provide such consent upon request from Licensee for any assignment to a Third Party who is acquiring the entire business of Licensee associated with performance under this Agreement, but Licensor reserves the right to deny consent if the Third Party is a Competitor of Licensor as that term is defined in Section ___.

Form 23.04C—Two-Notice Termination Provision

This Agreement may be terminated by either party upon written notice to the other party specifying a material breach by the other party of the provisions hereof. The non-breaching party may terminate this Agreement in the event the specified breach has not been cured within sixty (60) days after the written notice.

Form 23.04D—Single-Notice Termination Provision

This Agreement may be terminated by either party for a material breach by the other party of the provisions hereof. Such termination shall be effective sixty (60) days after written notice to the other party specifying the breach. If the specified breach is cured before the effective date of termination, the agreement will not be terminated and the notice thereof will have no effect.[1]


[1] It is important to note that such a single notice termination provision may affect termination upon notice, subject only to the passage of time, and cannot be revoked. See Duval Wiedmann, LLC v. Inforocket.com, Inc., 620 F.3d 496, 96 USPQ2d 1824 (5th Cir. 2010).
